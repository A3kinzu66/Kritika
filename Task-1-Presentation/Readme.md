For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page infographic on digital literacy.
Canva was chosen because it provides easy-to-use templates, icons, and design tools that simplify the process of creating visually appealing content without requiring prior design experience.
The infographic presents key aspects of digital literacy, including its definition, useful digital tools for students such as Google Docs and GitHub, safe internet practices like using strong passwords and avoiding phishing attacks, and the importance of maintaining a professional online presence through platforms like LinkedIn and proper email etiquette.
The design focuses on clarity and readability by organizing information into distinct sections with icons and short bullet points.
A consistent color scheme and minimal layout were used to ensure that the content is visually engaging and easy to understand.
One challenge I faced was balancing visual appeal with information density. 
It was difficult to include all relevant points without overcrowding the design. 
However, this process helped me understand the importance of concise communication and effective visual hierarchy in digital content creation.
